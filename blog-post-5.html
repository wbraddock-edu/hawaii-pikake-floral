Flower Arranging Basics for Island Homes

DIY · April 1, 2025 · 5 min read

You don't need to be a professional florist to fill your Hawaii home with beautiful flowers. With a few basic principles and access to the incredible tropical blooms available at island farmers markets and garden centers, you can create arrangements that bring joy to every room. Here's our guide to getting started.

Start with the Right Vessels

Island home décor tends to lean natural — rattan, wood, ceramic, and woven materials feel at home alongside tropical florals. Look for low, wide-mouthed vessels for horizontal "ikebana-inspired" arrangements, tall glass cylinders for dramatic single-stem displays, and vintage coconut shell bowls for casual, beachy arrangements. Thrift stores on the Windward side often have beautiful ceramic vessels at great prices.

The Rule of Three

The most fundamental principle in floral design is the "rule of three" — odd numbers of blooms almost always look more dynamic than even numbers. Arrange flowers at three different heights: a focal flower at the tallest point, secondary blooms at medium height, and low accent flowers or foliage to anchor the arrangement. This creates visual movement and interest.

Start with Foliage

Before adding a single flower, fill your vessel with tropical foliage. Ti leaves, monstera leaves, bird of paradise foliage, and fern fronds create a lush green foundation. This approach is called "building the framework" — once your greenery is in place, you can tuck flowers in for immediate impact without them drooping or looking sparse.

Island Flowers That Work for Beginners

Anthuriums are practically foolproof for first-time arrangers. They're long-lasting, structurally interesting, and available in warm colors that complement Hawaii's aesthetic. Ginger flowers (both white and red torch ginger) are bold and dramatic and require no special treatment. Bird of paradise, while striking, is also very easy to arrange — a single stem in a tall vase is a complete design statement. Heliconia, with their lobster-claw bracts in red and yellow, are available year-round at Kailua's farmers market.

Color Harmony for Tropical Arrangements

The most foolproof approach for beginners is monochromatic — all warm tones (reds, oranges, yellows) or all cool tones (whites, purples, blues). Once you're comfortable, try complementary color schemes: orange anthuriums with purple dendrobium orchids create a stunning contrast. For an effortlessly beautiful island look, use white blooms (plumeria, ginger, pikake) with deep tropical greens — this combination never fails.

Remember: the most important thing is to have fun and trust your instincts. Hawaii's flowers are so beautiful that even simple arrangements tend to be stunning. Start with what you love, learn as you go, and don't hesitate to stop by Pikake Floral for inspiration or a mini lesson.
